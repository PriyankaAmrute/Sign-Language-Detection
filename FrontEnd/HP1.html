INTRODUCTION


WHAT IS SIGN LANGUAGE?

Sign languages (also known as signed languages) are languages that use the visual-manual modality to convey meaning, instead of spoken words. Sign languages are expressed through manual articulation in combination with non-manual markers. Sign languages are full-fledged natural languages with their own grammar and lexicon.Sign languages are not universal and are usually not mutually intelligible, although there are also similarities among different sign languages
Non-verbal manner conveys and communicates our views, emotions, and thoughts visually through sign language. Hence sign language is the primary mode of communication between the hearing and vocally impaired population. A sign language comprises specific hand gestures, facial expression, lip movements, shapes, or signals produced by body movements.



WHAT OUR PROJECT DOES?

Sign language interpreter refers to the conversion of these gestures into words or alphabets of existing formally spoken languages. Thus, conversion of sign language into words by an algorithm or a model can help bridge the gap between people with hearing or speaking impairment and the rest of the world.
This program will take hand gestures as an input through camera (webcam) and detect those hand gestures. After detection it will map those gestures with our database and find its proper match i.e. the program will predict what the person is trying to convey and appropriately convert sign language/hand gestures into texts. This text will be labelled as an output on the screen.
